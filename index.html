How do we stop over thinking?

You only over think because you try to get a solution to the problem that you like more.
Unaccepting of what is the most direct, and often most beneficial, way to solve your problem you think and think and think in the hopes of getting an easier, more comfortable and less confrontational solution.
That is when over thinking comes into play. Take a deep Breath. Relax. And then boil the problem down to its simplest form. Try to find a way to get to action in as few steps as possible. I. E. You are low on money. Find a second job.
Not necessarily the easiest solution, nor the most comfortable one, but a solution nonetheless, and most often the most effective one. The point is to get yourself to do something and to follow a path. While that second job may not be the ideal solution it at least is a starting point!
It‚Äôs something you can look for while you come up with something maybe better. You can always switch your objective, but if you do not do anything at all nothing will change! In this sense, the only way to overcome overthinking is by converting it into action.
Do something, anything. It will get you much further than thinking about it nonstop.

In recent years, many cities have become conscious of something called the"broken window syndrome".Often, when a building is abandoned, the first thing that happens is somebody breaks the windows. Those broken windows become a clue, an indicator to others, that the property has become vulnerable.
Ifthe windows are not repaired promptly, a predictable cycle of decline follows‚Äîvagrants move in, fires are started, plumbing fixtures are stolen, and so on.
